London Icon

Big Ben & Palace of Westminster

The legendary 96-meter clock tower officially named Elizabeth Tower. Features four 7-meter diameter clock faces. Part of the Palace of Westminster, home to the UK Parliament since 1295.

History
Engineering Marvel

Tower Bridge

Victorian bascule bridge (1894) with unique hydraulic lift system. High-level walkways at 42m offer Thames panoramas. Lifts approximately 800 times a year for river traffic.

Tours
City Panorama

London Eye

Europe's tallest Ferris wheel (135m). 32 capsules represent London's boroughs. Complete rotation takes 30 minutes with 40km visibility. Hosted over 85 million visitors since 2000.

Tickets
Royal Residence

Buckingham Palace

Official London residence of UK monarchs since 1837. 775 rooms including 52 royal bedrooms. Famous for 45-minute Changing the Guard ceremony with military band.

History Treasury

British Museum

World's second most visited museum (6.8M annual visitors). 8 million artifacts spanning 2 million years. Highlights: Rosetta Stone, Elgin Marbles, Egyptian mummies.

Collections
Architectural Masterpiece

St Paul's Cathedral

Principal Anglican cathedral (1675-1710) standing 111m tall. 65,000-ton dome inspired global architecture. Hosted Prince Charles' wedding and Winston Churchill's funeral.
